Importance of a Side Hustle

Young people such as university students still attending or graduates are being surprised by the difficulty of finding a career path. Universities are pumping our graduates which then have to wait a few years before they get a entry level job in their desired role, why is that ? It is a combination of things such as our parents teaching outdated strategies for building a life and the pressure from pop culture to stay on a certain path. Universities prioritize increasing cashflow rather than ensuring employment for the graduates. So, what should you do ? In this article you will read some tips which can give you a different perspective and hopefully provide you with more personal agency. You should do a side hustle. It is very mundane advice because the phrase “side hustle” is usually associated with selling candy in middle school or re selling garbage with a big margin. But ideally a side hustle is a way of you conducting business with other members of society in your free time, without you ruining your focus on school, work or family life. People always say “I don’t have time”, but life is not about having or not having time, it’s about making time for things that are important to you. So allocate a little time of your life to learn about something and then be useful to others. It’s all about learning the skill rather than bringing in a cashflow. So have a pro-bono business model. Edit videos for your local company, help with some gardening for your neighbor, help your grandma design a brochure for her knitting club, the list goes on and on. By dedicating your time to side hustles you learn hard skills and experience, something that university and school rarely provide. You don’t have to always charge money for your services since your main goal is to learn the skill. So in case you a pro-bono business model, keep the costs as low as possible. If you do decide to attend a university, do not expect it to turn you into a different person. For every genius professor there are hundreds of mundane professors which simply lack real life experience necessary to instruct new generations. It is still advisable to be respectful and humble on every occasion. Treat university as one big network event. That is where you meet people with whom you will have the opportunity to do business with in the future. Don’t be Machiavelli, value people for their character but also understand that your university friends which are getting drunk 3 times a week probably won’t be the best business partners after graduation. You want to save your university projects in an archive, especially presentations. These are things you will be able to showcase in the future during job interviews. Having concrete proof of your skills is the best way to prove yourself to a hiring manager. And finally, just understand that it is simply hard and a lot of things depend on connections and your integrity. There is a small demand for workers and a very high supply of university graduates. Embrace this crazy competition and try to find a positive angle to it. Contrary to what older generations like to say, young people are not lazy. Many restaurant waiters and cleaners are university educated, they just had to get money doing low level work to make ends meet. Nowadays many normal things like housing or private transport or even going on vacation have become a luxury that will cost you most of your net worth. It’s healthy for the mind to have a “what do I need to do to achieve this” mentality when faced with a seemingly difficult task. At the end of the day life is about learning and having a good time so do your very best, and, regardless of the results you will feel fulfilled.
